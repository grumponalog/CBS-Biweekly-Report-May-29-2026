03

Spotlight: Blog strategy reset

Why this spotlight
Two patterns showed up clearly this period: LinkedIn drives real traffic that bounces fast, and most blog readers leave within a couple of paragraphs. We need to create more value and give the reader more jumping-off points before they click away.
What's changing
  • 01TLDR section (less than 250 words) at the top of every blog. Quotable lines double as fuel for AI search results.
  • 02CTAs above the fold. Capture the reader before they bounce, not after they finish reading.
  • 03Internal links throughout each blog, especially to relevant middle-of-funnel content. The next read is one click away.
  • 04Word count isn't the lever with the May 21 update — originality and genuine authorship are. The update rewards practitioner POV; a 500-word piece in a real voice ranks higher than a 2,500-word AI-thin or aggregated piece. Practical guideline: blog posts in the 800–1,200 range when you're publishing practitioner POV (case studies, frameworks, hard-won lessons); shorter is fine if the voice is genuinely yours and the content is novel.
  • 05One topic per blog. No more "alphabet soup."
  • 06AI owner-perspective scan removes client names and flags brand-misalignment or anything potentially controversial before it ships.
  • 07Review summary sent to the approver instead of the full blog. ~3-minute approval, not 30.

04

Marketing briefing

May 29, 2026

Google's AI Overviews and AI Mode just expanded to every U.S. search query.

Landed May 21, 2026
What happened

Google turned on AI-generated answers above the regular search results for every search in the U.S. People now see an AI answer first, the list of websites second.

What it means

AI answers pull from pages that are structured: clear summaries, short paragraphs, named sections, and a small bit of code that tells AI "this is a Q&A." Long rambling blogs lose. Tight, expert-written pages get cited.

What it means for CBS

The blog reset shipping this period is now doubly important. The new blog format creates more citable content for AI Mode and protects against the Google algorithm sorting CBS content as not relevant to the audience.

Glossary & Method

Plain-English definitions of every term and number used in this report.
Lead / SQL (working definition)
A verified contact-form submission, or a sales meeting booked through the CBS sales process.
MQL · Marketing Qualified Lead
A lead that meets the agreed scoring rubric (account fit + behavioral signal). Definition pending.
TOFU · MOFU · BOFU
Top / Middle / Bottom of Funnel. Used here because CBS uses them internally.
Session
One visitor showing up at the CBS website. Multiple pageviews by the same person within 30 minutes count as one session.
Engaged session
A session that lasted at least 10 seconds, fired a conversion event, or had ≥2 pageviews.
Engagement rate
Percentage of sessions that count as engaged.
ABM · Account-Based Marketing
Outreach treating a small list of named accounts as a market of one. "Send-Cake" is the brand alias.
Marketing Briefing
A targeted research brief produced for a named prospect. Proof of competence before the first conversation.
Take-Don't-Give Podcast
An interview format where the guest contributes the expertise; we get content, warm intros, and trust.
FAQ schema
Structured data markup on a page that helps AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Mode) cite the page as a source.
